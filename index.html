Short Bio

Kai Wu received the B.S. degree in intelligent science and technology and the Ph.D. degree in circuits and systems from Xidian University, Xi’an, China, in 2015 and 2020, respectively. He is currently an Associate Professor with the School of Artificial Intelligence, Xidian University. His current research interests include complex system modeling, evolutionary computation, complex networks, and data mining.

He is the chair of IEEE Symposium 2021 on Multi-agent System Coordination and Optimization.

He is the chair of IEEE Symposium 2020 on Multi-agent System Coordination and Optimization.

He is the chair of The 2nd International Conference on Industrial Artificial Intelligence, Invited Session: Industrial Artificial Intelligence for Time Series Data in Large-scale Complex Systems.

Research Topic

The main research directions are complex system modeling and its application, evolutionary optimization and learning, time series analysis, and adversarial machine learning.

  • 1. Data-driven multi-scenario system intelligent modeling method and its application: mainly use the designed model to learn and approximate a certain function of the complex system, and then use the learned model to complete various tasks. The following directions are involved:

    • 1.1. Modeling of complex systems based on fuzzy cognitive maps: Use the interpretability and abstraction of fuzzy cognitive maps to learn the knowledge of complex system representations to achieve the effect of simulating complex system functions.

    • 1.2. Network reconstruction: Use the dynamic characteristics of the complex system to learn the network structure relationship of the key factors in the complex system according to the knowledge of the complex system representation to achieve the purpose of controlling the complex system.

    • 1.3. Causal inference.

    • 1.4. Various applications based on the built model: Mainly study time series forecasting, time series classification, and fault detection in complex systems.

  • 2. Evolutionary optimization and learning: Solve optimization problems with complex structures, such as non-convex, non-differentiable and non-continuous problems with many local optimal solutions. The following directions are involved:

    • 2.1. Large-scale optimization problems: There are high-dimensional situations in most practical problems, and the main research is how to design evolutionary algorithms to solve actual large-scale optimization problems.

    • 2.2. Expernsive optimization problem: In most practical problems, the objective function estimation is expensive. The main research is how to design evolutionary algorithms to solve the actual costly problem optimization problem.

    • 2.3. Evolutionary multi-task optimization: mainly discusses the possibility of knowledge transfer in various tasks in network science.

    • 2.4. Evolutionary deep learning: mainly discusses the possibility of combining deep learning and evolution to break through their shortcomings.

  • 3. Time series analysis: Solve various tasks where the characterization data is time series. The application scenario is the design of the subsequent intelligent operation and maintenance plan for fault detection and prediction in large-scale industrial scenarios. The anomaly detection on the aero engine and high-speed rail track data has been completed, involving the following directions:

    • 3.1. Time series classification: study how to efficiently deal with time series classification problems in extreme scenarios such as large-scale, noisy, and class imbalance.

    • 3.2. Time series forecasting: study how to efficiently perform time series forecasting in extreme scenarios such as large-scale, noisy, and multi-step forecasting.

    • 3.3. Time series clustering: study how to efficiently perform time series clustering in large-scale, noisy and other extreme scenarios.

    • 3.4. Evolutionary deep learning: mainly discusses the possibility of combining deep learning and evolution to break through their shortcomings.

  • 4. Time series analysis: Solve various tasks where the characterization data is time series. The application scenario is the design of the subsequent intelligent operation and maintenance plan for fault detection and prediction in large-scale industrial scenarios. The anomaly detection on the aero engine and high-speed rail track data has been completed, involving the following directions:

    • 4.1. Time series classification: study how to efficiently deal with time series classification problems in extreme scenarios such as large-scale, noisy, and class imbalance.

    • 4.2. Time series forecasting: study how to efficiently perform time series forecasting in extreme scenarios such as large-scale, noisy, and multi-step forecasting.

    • 4.3. Time series clustering: study how to efficiently perform time series clustering in large-scale, noisy and other extreme scenarios.

    • 4.4. Time series modeling: study how to efficiently perform time series modeling in extreme scenarios such as large-scale and noise.

    • 4.5. Anomaly detection based on time series: study how to efficiently detect abnormal data from time series.

    • 5. Adversarial machine learning: The application direction is the confrontation in the process of modeling complex systems, making the corresponding model invalid or having stronger robustness. The following directions are involved:

      • 5.1. Adversarial learning in complex system modeling.

      • 5.2. Adversarial learning on evolutionary optimization.

      • 5.3. Time series clustering: study how to efficiently perform time series clustering in large-scale, noisy and other extreme scenarios.

      • 5.4. Time series modeling: study how to efficiently perform time series modeling in extreme scenarios such as large-scale and noise.

      • 5.5. Adversarial learning on time series tasks.
