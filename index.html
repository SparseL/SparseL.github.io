Short Bio

Kai Wu received the B.S. degree in intelligent science and technology and the Ph.D. degree in circuits and systems from Xidian University, Xi’an, China, in 2015 and 2020, respectively. He is currently an Associate Professor with the School of Artificial Intelligence, Xidian University. His current research interests include complex system modeling, evolutionary computation, complex networks, and data mining.

He is the chair of IEEE Symposium 2022 on Multi-agent System Coordination and Optimization.

He is the chair of IEEE Symposium 2021 on Multi-agent System Coordination and Optimization.

He is the chair of IEEE Symposium 2020 on Multi-agent System Coordination and Optimization.

He is the chair of The 2nd International Conference on Industrial Artificial Intelligence, Invited Session: Industrial Artificial Intelligence for Time Series Data in Large-scale Complex Systems.

Research Topic

The main research directions are complex system modeling and its applications, evolutionary optimization and learning, time series analysis, and adversarial machine learning.

  • 1. AI for Network Sciences

    • 1.1. Causal inference.

    • 1.2. Higher Order Networks, Hypergraphs, Network Reconstruction, Community Detection, Influence Maximization, Network Augmentation, Network Denoising, Network Robustness, Fuzzy Cognitive Maps.

  • 2. Deep Learning/Generative Model for Evolutionary Algorithm

    • 2.1. Machine Learning Assisted Evolutionary Optimization: Machine learning meet Evolutionary Agorithms

    • 2.2. Deep Network Evolutionary Optimization: Meta learning + Evolutionary Agorithms > 2

    • 2.3. Kinetic analysis of evolutionary optimization: Graph for EA

    • 2.4. Traditional evolutionary optimization: large-scale optimization problems, expensive optimization problems, evolutionary multi-task learning, combinatorial optimization problems, evolutionary deep learning

  • 3. Deep Learning for Time Series

    • 3.1. Neural ODE + Graph

    • 3.2. Time series classification, time series forecasting, time series clustering, time series modeling, time series anomaly detection, time series super-resolution, time series generation, time series interpolation, time series imputation

  • 4. Adversarial Machine Learning

    • 4.1. Adversarial Learning on Network Tasks

    • 4.2. Adversarial Learning on Evolutionary Optimization

    • 4.3. Adversarial Learning on Time Series Tasks
